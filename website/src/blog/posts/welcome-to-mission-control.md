Every week someone shows us a demo of an AI that talks beautifully about work. It summarizes the meeting, drafts the plan, explains the strategy. Then the demo ends, and a person opens twelve tabs and does the actual work by hand.

We built Houston because we think that gap is the whole game. Not smarter answers. **Finished work.**

## What Houston is

Houston is a desktop app with AI agents that do real tasks in your real tools. A sales rep that follows up with the contacts you met at an event. A personal assistant that sorts your inbox and books your meetings. Agents that connect to more than a thousand apps, run on a schedule, and come back to you with outcomes, not suggestions.

It is free and open source. You can read every line of it on [GitHub](https://github.com/gethouston/houston).

## What we believe

A few convictions drive what we ship:

- **Doing beats advising.** An agent that sends the follow-up is worth ten that draft it. We measure Houston by tasks completed, not tokens generated.
- **Non-technical people are the point.** You should not need to know what an API is to put agents to work. If a feature requires reading documentation, it is not done.
- **Your work should stay yours.** Houston runs on your computer, with your accounts, under your control. Open source is not a marketing line; it is how you can trust an agent with your inbox.
- **Software should bend.** Products used to be take-it-or-leave-it. Agents change that: your tools can finally adapt to how you work instead of the other way around. That idea runs deep for us; the long version is in [our vision essay](/vision/).
